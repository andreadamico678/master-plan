# Why EIT Digital Didn't Make the Cut

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This postmortem documents the strategic decision to officially drop the EIT Digital track from the Master Plan. It serves as a permanent reference to prevent planning regression and lock in the 100% UniBo + Erasmus+ trajectory.

---

## The Strategic Breakdown

### 🔴 Reason 1: The Geographic Trap
The desired technical track, **Intelligent Chips and Systems (ICS)**, has a severely restricted geographic layout. It does not have partner universities in the primary target countries (**Netherlands, Sweden, Germany**).
*   **The Problem:** The northernmost academic node for the ICS track is Finland.
*   **The Fallout:** While Finland has a strong tech sector, it fails the long-term strategic requirement for the post-graduation exit strategy. Specifically, Finnish residency rules and language barriers are highly disadvantageous for achieving rapid local citizenship or transitioning into European law enforcement / high-tier cyber intelligence agencies compared to the Netherlands (NFI/Europol) or Sweden/Germany.

### 🟡 Reason 2: Curriculum Compromise
To force a target geography (like Sweden or Germany) via the EIT Digital framework, we would be forced to pivot away from the **ICS** track and instead enroll in the **Autonomous Systems (AUS)** track.
*   **The Problem:** The AUS track dilutes the core technical focus of the Master Plan.
*   **The Fallout:** Instead of a pure, highly specialized focus on **Embedded Systems, Edge AI, and Hardware-Level Reverse Engineering (Hardware Forensics)**, the curriculum would shift toward robotics, control systems, and high-level autonomous navigation. This dilution weakens the candidate profile for elite semiconductor firms (ASML, NXP) and cyber-intel agencies.

### 🔵 Reason 3: Financial & Bureaucratic Risk
The EIT Digital program requires competing for highly selective merit-based scholarships or taking on immediate student debt/loans.
*   **The Problem:** Lack of financial guarantees.
*   **The Fallout:** Entering a multi-country program with tuition fees up to €15,000/year and high relocation costs introduces severe financial stress and risk. In contrast, the **UniBo LM-32 + ER.GO scholarship** combination provides:
    *   **100% guaranteed financial safety** via the Italian regional welfare system.
    *   Full tuition exemption and ~€8,130 annual cash grant.
    *   Free or highly subsidized student housing in Cesena.
    *   Zero student debt upon graduation.

---

## Conclusion: The "Full UniBo" Supremacy

By discarding EIT Digital, we:
1.  **Defuse the November 2026 Timebomb:** Eliminate the high-stress deadline that was forcing a premature and hasty portfolio rush.
2.  **Ensure 100% Financial Safety:** Rely on a guaranteed, stable national system (ER.GO) instead of high-risk merit competition or debt.
3.  **Unlock Ultimate Geographic Flexibility:** Use the **Erasmus+ / Bando Tesi all'Estero** bilateral agreements to target the absolute best universities (TU Eindhoven, TU Delft, Chalmers, TU Munich) for a 6-month thesis internship, translating directly into local, high-paying jobs without compromising the curriculum.
