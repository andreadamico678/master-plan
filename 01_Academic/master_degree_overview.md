# Master's Degree Overview: Intelligent Embedded Systems (LM-32)

**Institution**: University of Bologna — Campus Cesena
**Degree class**: LM-32 (Computer Systems Engineering)
**Target intake**: September 2028
**Source**: [UniBo Official Course Structure](https://corsi.unibo.it/2cycle/ComputerScienceEngineering/course-structure-diagram/piani-didattici/2025/5850/A62/000/2025)

---

## Why This Program

The LM-32 at UniBo Cesena is the most aligned Master's degree available in the Italian public university system for the target career path (Embedded Systems / Edge AI Engineer, Northern Europe). Key selection factors:

- **Name-to-role alignment**: "Intelligent Embedded Systems" maps directly to job titles at NXP, Nokia, and Nordic Semiconductor
- **Curriculum depth**: first year is entirely compulsory and covers Embedded Systems & IoT, Cybersecurity, Distributed Systems, and Intelligent Systems Engineering — all directly applicable to the technical portfolio
- **EIT Digital track availability**: while the program offers a 1st year at UniBo and 2nd year abroad, this path is deprioritized due to high financial requirements.
- **Thesis flexibility**: can be completed via Erasmus+ Traineeship or company internship, enabling a structured entry into a Northern European employer

## Program Structure

### Year 1 — Compulsory (48 CFU)

| Course | Relevance to Career Target |
| :----- | :------------------------- |
| Embedded Systems and IoT | Core — directly extends the technical portfolio |
| Embedded Sensors | Core — sensor interfacing and signal conditioning at graduate level |
| Intelligent Systems Engineering | Core — formal ML/AI methods for constrained systems |
| Cybersecurity | Relevant — extends CAN bus IDS research into a formal security context |
| Distributed Systems | Supporting — edge-cloud architecture, relevant for IoT deployment |
| Fundamentals of Management | Degree requirement |
| Organization, Teams and Digital Leadership | Degree requirement |
| Programmable Networking | Supporting — SDN/NFV context for network-connected embedded nodes |

**Elective choice (6 CFU, Local Track)**: Machine Learning and Data Mining — deepens the Edge AI layer of the portfolio.

**Guided choice (6 CFU, Local Track)**: Industry 4.0 — relevant to automotive and industrial embedded systems context.

### Year 2 — Guided Choice (24 CFU)

Second year composition depends on track selected (see below). Key courses of interest:

- **Smart Vehicular Systems** — directly relevant to automotive embedded / CAN bus background
- **Intelligent Robotic Systems** — applicable if trajectory shifts toward robotics/actuator control
- **Big Data / Operational Analytics** — supports large-scale telemetry and fleet-level anomaly detection

### Final Examination (30 CFU)

Thesis prepared locally, via Erasmus+ Traineeship, or in partnership with a company.
**Target**: industry-partnered thesis at a Northern European company (NXP Eindhoven, Nokia, Bosch) as the primary employment entry point.

---

## Track Selection

| Track | Structure | Strategic fit |
| :---- | :-------- | :------------ |
| **Local Track** | 2 years at UniBo Cesena | Simpler logistics; lower cost; thesis abroad still possible via Erasmus+ |
| **EIT Digital Track** | Year 1 UniBo → Year 2 abroad | Built-in international exposure; high financial burden (tuition/living costs); complex logistics |

**Final Decision**: **Local Track** with Erasmus+ Traineeship thesis.
> [!IMPORTANT]
> The EIT Digital Track is formally excluded due to extreme financial constraints (ISEE < 5,000 EUR). The Local Track combined with Erasmus+ grants provides a sustainable path to international exposure without the prohibitive costs of the EIT program.
